Combined Meaning:


An engineering student is defined as an individual pursuing education in engineering or engineering technology, focusing on excellence in instruction, research, and technological education to contribute to society.

Engineering, like the physical sciences, relies heavily on journal articles and, to a much lesser extent, on books. The field is different from many others, though, in the heavy use of conference proceedings as an information source. Students must be comfortable citing both print and online conference papers, as well as papers presented but not published. Additionally, technical reports, standards, patents, and product information are frequently cited in engineering and students need to be competent in incorporating citations to these material types in their work. Use of other information sources tends to vary depending on the sub-field of engineering under consideration: for example, computer engineering will rely more heavily on software code than other areas, while chemical engineers are more likely to use sources like Material Data Safety Sheets (MSDS). Seperately

Student

  1. a person formally engaged in learning, especially one enrolled in a school or college; pupil: a student at Yale.
  2. any person who studies, investigates, or examines thoughtfully: a student of human nature.

Engineer

  1. a person trained and skilled in the design, construction, and use of engines or machines, or in any of various branches of engineering: a mechanical engineer; a civil engineer.
  2. a person who operates or is in charge of an engine.
